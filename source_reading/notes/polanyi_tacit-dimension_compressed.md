## Michael Polanyi -- *The Tacit Dimension* (1966)

### The move

All knowing has a from-to structure: we attend *from* subsidiary particulars we cannot specify *to* a focal whole we can. The blind man's stick, the diagnostician's eye, the scientist's hunch -- same structure. Formalization is always parasitic on this tacit layer; any program to make all knowledge explicit is self-defeating, because the act of applying the formalism is itself tacit. Extends this into biology (living things are hierarchies of levels, each controlling boundary conditions left open by the one below) and into politics (a free society of explorers requires tradition and authority as the medium through which tacit knowledge is transmitted).

### Passages that stuck

1. "we can know more than we can tell" (p. 4) [anchor -- the sentence the whole edifice rests on]

2. "it is not by looking at things, but by dwelling in them, that we understand their joint meaning" (p. 18) [epistemological -- indwelling as the mode of all comprehension]

3. "the kind of lucidity which destroys its subject matter" (p. 25) [diagnostic -- what happens when you formalize what can't be formalized]

4. "You cannot derive a vocabulary from phonetics; you cannot derive the grammar of a language from its vocabulary; a correct use of grammar does not account for good style; and a good style does not provide the content of a piece of prose" (p. 36) [structural -- the irreducibility of levels, demonstrated in one sentence]

5. "Originality is commanded at every stage by a sense of responsibility for advancing the growth of truth in men's minds. Its freedom is perfect service" (p. 77) [moral -- the alternative to both existentialist self-determination and positivist objectivism]

### Where it connects

- **Sennett's *Craftsman*** is Polanyi with sociological extension. The hand teaches the head = the from-to structure applied to craft. Sennett cites Polanyi extensively.
- **Franklin's holistic/prescriptive distinction** is Polanyi's dual control applied to labor. Holistic: the worker controls the boundary conditions. Prescriptive: the process fixes them from above.
- **Flyvbjerg's phronesis** is Polanyi's tacit knowing applied to social science method. The argument that practical wisdom can't be reduced to rules is direct Polanyi.
- **Rao's legibility** is what happens when Polanyi's "destructive lucidity" is wielded by institutional power -- the state that can't read a complex reality destroys it with simplification.
- **Ingold's *Making*** and **Korn's *Why We Make Things*** are the practice side of Polanyi's indwelling. Making as a way of knowing, the workshop as a place where tacit knowledge lives.

### What it does NOT say

Nothing about power or exclusion. Who gets access to the apprenticeships through which tacit knowledge is transmitted? The "society of explorers" is an idealized scientific community with no account of who is kept out. The evolutionary speculation in chapters 2--3 (emergence as creative agency, "a field of potentialities") is suggestive but not argued -- asserted on structural analogy, not evidence. The diagnosis of skepticism-plus-perfectionism is historically specific to Cold War liberalism; the mapping onto contemporary conditions is left to the reader.
