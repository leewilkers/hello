This is a very long essay (~30,000 words) with extensive images and diagrams. It covers information software design, arguing that most software should be designed as context-sensitive information graphics rather than interactive manipulation tools. Major sections include discussions of information software vs. manipulation software, context (environment, history, interaction), graphic design as the primary interface, and reducing the need for interaction.
