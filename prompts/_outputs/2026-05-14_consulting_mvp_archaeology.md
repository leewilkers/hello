# Consulting

*[Lee writes the lede here. Per project CLAUDE.md. Default fallback if skipped: "I take consulting work through LW Research and Advisory, LLC."]*

*realist management*, *useful disagreement*, *strategic unplanning*
*(or a consulting-specific triplet — open question, see §3 of archaeology doc; cite all or cite none)*

Most global health programs run two versions of themselves: the one in the reporting documents, and the one being delivered at the door. The two versions do not always agree, and in most programs no one is responsible for closing the gap between them. Programs end up defending the reporting version, because the reporting version is what gets audited – even when the field has quietly worked out something better.

I work between those two versions.

**Problems I've been paid to think about** *[new — Lee redline: pick public-safe subset of the 9 from source packet]*

- Whether disease surveillance counts as human subjects research
- What "equity" actually meant when there weren't enough COVID vaccines
- How to get meaningful consent when medicating an entire village
- Whether convincing someone until they agree is coercion or persuasion
- Who gets authorship credit when 50 people contributed across 6 countries
- What refusal looks like when the only mechanism is not showing up

**On frameworks.** "Wicked problems" is a frame for hard problems. The term has been used too many ways to mean much. Once I've called a problem wicked, I know it is hard. I do not know what kind of hard, or what to do next. Frameworks – VUCA, wicked, whatever is current – are useful for talking about problems. They are less useful for working through them. The specifics of any particular program are always more granular than the label that gets applied to it.

**Getting started.** If you would like to work together, send me a short or long note about the problem you're working on. Email or DM is fine. I'll ask follow-up questions to figure out whether I can be useful before either of us puts time or money down. The more context you share, the better I can dig in beforehand, and the more useful our first call will be.

Most engagements start with a paid "thought sparring" conversation: $350 for a 60–90 minute call. I send a few prep questions a day or two ahead. The call helps me understand the situation and figure out the next useful move, while giving you a sense of whether you're [in good hands](https://stephango.com/in-good-hands). If $350 is too much, write anyway – depending on your situation, there's flexibility on rate for early-stage or public-interest work. Beyond the first call, we agree scope and rate in writing.

`EMAIL` *[address]*
