## Lee Raw Riff

```text
I find myself at a very unique Lagrange point between disciplines & perspectives; the value I offer is this unique perspective.

I’ve gathered a suite of (my favorite / the best) “tools” along the way, and have made a few of my own. Through blood, sweat, and tears, I’ve acquired a working knowledge of these tools. I’ve also learned the difference between a tool-shaped problem and a human-shaped problem.  I can help you sort of the difference.

For complicated problems, I’ve found that people often already have the answers themselves. All that’s usually required is "bending the frame” to see things in a new, clearer way.  Bending the frame well–with a human touch–is a skill. That’s why you pay me.
```

```text
I tend to work at the Lagrange point between disciplines: close enough to feel the pull of each one, far enough out to see what they are doing to each other.

I’ve collected tools along the way: research methods, ethics frameworks, facilitation moves, writing habits, protocols, checklists, ways of reading a room. Some I inherited. Some I made because the available ones did not quite fit.

The useful thing is judgment about the tools. Some problems are tool-shaped. Others are human-shaped, and get worse when someone brings in another template. I can help you sort out the difference.

In complicated work, people often already know more than they can say. The task is bending the frame: changing the angle of the document, the question, the meeting, or the room until the thing comes into view. Doing this kind of frame-bending well, without sanding off the human texture, is a skill.

That’s why you pay me.
```
